About Me

Hello and thanks for stopping by! My name is Erfan(Erfun) Zabeh (عرفان ذابح). I am a Forth year PhD student at Columbia University and I have been lucky to be advised by Joshua Jacobs and Jackie Gottlieb. I started my PhD as a RISE Prize Fellow at the Biomedical Engineering Department and Zuckerman Mind Brain Behavior Institute at Columbia University. My research is influenced by a fundamental question in neuroscience: How do different brain areas coordinate their activities to support complex behaviors? To this end, I work on high-resolution measurements of cortical traveling waves for reinforcement learning and decision-making.

Prior to joining Columbia, I was a research assistant at the School of Cognitive Sciences of IPM Institute For Research In Fundamental Sciences in Tehran. I was also the recipient of the Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship in computational neuroscience at SUNY College of Optometry. I received my BSc with a major in Electrical Engineering and two minors in Physics and Mathematics from the Sharif University of Technology. I'm passionate about Physics and enjoys teaching it. An International Astronomy and Astrophysics Olympiad medalist himself, I went coaching high school students during my college years. During my free time, I play soccer(midfielder) and performs Tar, a traditional Persian instrument.

Some evidence of me!

Captured on a mesmerizing "Yalda night" by a great friend in Manhattan in 2022, this portrait reminds me of the enchanting spirit of this cultural celebration. Yalda, also known as "Shab-e Yalda", is an ancient multi-culrtural festival celebrated on the longest night of the year, symbolizing the triumph of light over darkness. Families and friends come together, sharing laughter, stories, and traditional foods. As pomegranates, watermelons, and nuts adorn the table, the vibrant colors and joyful expressions in this portrait radiate the warmth and togetherness that Yalda brings.
