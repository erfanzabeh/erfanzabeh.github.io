Duality of Experiemnt vs Theory!

Once upon a time, as a curious kid, I dreamed of making telescopes. Little did I know about optics—didn't even know it was a thing! But I was determined to figure it out, just like Galileo in his experimental days.

Galileo, too, was all about hands-on experiments. Back then, folks weren't sure whether optics or experiments came first. But let's fast forward a bit. Eventually, the science of optics made its grand entrance. I realized that sometimes, you need to try things out before understanding the science behind them.

Fast forward again to today. We're not crafting telescopes, but diving into the mysteries of the brain. Now, here's the tricky part: Should we start with theories or experiments? Well, turns out, it's not about picking sides. It's more like a dance where both partners—theory and experiment—work together.

In our modern world, where theories have reached new heights, it's like having a treasure map before going on an adventure. So, armed with theories, the kid in me sets out on a scientific quest. The dance of theory and experiment isn't a puzzle anymore; it's a partnership, a teamwork that propels us forward in our endless pursuit of understanding. And that, my friend, is the tale of experiments, theories, and the humble dance of science.
