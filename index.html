About

I received my PhD in Computational Neuroscience and conduct research at the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning. My work focuses on understanding traveling waves in neural networks, how the brain encodes spatial information, and the role of oscillations in cognitive processing.

I am committed to making research accessible and engaging. I teach courses on computational neuroscience and contribute to open science initiatives through publications and teaching materials.

Research Interests

  • Traveling waves in neural networks and their role in cognition
  • Spatial representation and path integration
  • Neural oscillations and their functional significance
  • Computational models of brain function
  • Decision-making and reward processing
