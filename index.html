Erfan Zabeh

I am a Postdoctoral Research Scientist at the Center for Theoretical Neuroscience, Zuckerman Mind Brain Behavior Institute at Columbia University, working with Larry F. Abbott.

With tens of billions of parameters, today's foundational models approach the scale of biological neural circuits‚Äîand the theoretical study of artificial and natural intelligence is converging. I use theory as a bridge between the two, interchangeably drawing insights from neuroscience and machine learning to uncover the principles that govern intelligent behavior in both biological brains and AI systems.

I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from Columbia University in the Zuckerman Institute, where I was advised by Joshua Jacobs and Jacqueline Gottlieb, and started as a RISE Prize Fellow. Before that, I studied Electrical Engineering and Physics at Sharif University of Technology, and was a medalist of the International Olympiad on Astronomy and Astrophysics.

My publications are listed below, or see Google Scholar. My CV is here.

Research Program

As artificial neural networks swell to billions of parameters, the theoretical neuroscience toolkit‚Äîbuilt to analyze billions of synapses‚Äîbecomes essential for understanding and advancing today's large language models and foundational models. My research asks: How do neural structures in space (micro-connectomes, traveling-wave modes) and rules in time (plasticity, drift) jointly carve high-dimensional parameter spaces into manifolds that support robust, curiosity-driven intelligence?

LLMs in Dry Labs: The dream of neuroscience is a clear picture of the brain during behavior‚Äîcell-type-specific recordings, synaptic-level connectomes, whole-brain imaging. We're getting closer, but the brain remains stubbornly opaque. In parallel, artificial neural networks are racing ahead: they already exhibit complex, human-like behaviors while granting us full access to every weight, activation, and gradient‚Äîtangible parameters producing intangible complexity. For the first time, we can study intelligence the way physicists study controlled systems‚Äîwith complete observability and unlimited intervention. LLMs are the new model organisms of cognition, and computational theory is the microscope. I'm building a research program around this vision‚Äîif it resonates with you, let's talk or explore some ongoing projects.
